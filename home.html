Project Overview

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally. This project uses machine learning to predict mortality caused by heart failure using clinical records from 299 patients with 12 medical features.

The goal is to support early risk detection and help healthcare professionals identify high-risk patients earlier.

Methodology

Data Preprocessing

  • Scaling: StandardScaler used to normalize numerical features.
  • Balancing: SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) applied to correct dataset imbalance.
  • Splitting: 80/20 Train-Test split with stratification.

Model Selection

Random Forest Classifier was selected due to its robustness and strong performance on structured datasets.

  • Ensemble Method: Reduces overfitting compared to a single decision tree.
  • Non-Linear Capability: Captures complex relationships between features.
  • Optimized Hyperparameters: n_estimators=100, max_depth=10.
